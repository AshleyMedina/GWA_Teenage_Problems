Dealing with depression and anxiety

Understanding what seems insurmountable

Depression and anxiety are overwhelming illnesses. Despite being extremely common diseases that, according to the National Institute of Health, effect millions of Americans every year, they are often difficult to recognize and even more difficult to talk about. Those who suspect they suffer from depression or anxiety might feel uncomfortable with the idea of being different from their peers, or they might blame themselves for their negative emotions and reactions.

However, an important part of taking care of one's health is dealing with the possibility of mental disorders. There is nothing wrong with having these illnesses, and everyone can suffer from them. If you suspect that you suffer from depression or anxiety, check out the links below for more information and help.

The game below, Elude, was created by the MIT Game Lab as an educational tool to raise awareness for depression. It is a valuable tool that can help others understand the frustration and sadnesss that comes with the illness. As the website states,

"Life is a never-ending struggle, full of rising and falling moods. Elude mirrors this struggle against the rising tide of depression, and the search for a path to happiness. Yet happiness remains elusive. Again and again, losing passion for anything in life, you plunge into depression. Only by continually calling out to the world can you find experiences that resonate, allowing you to ascend into happiness."
